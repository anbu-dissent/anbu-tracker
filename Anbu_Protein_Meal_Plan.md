# Anbu — Eggetarian High-Protein Meal Plan

**Profile:** 100 kg, office daily, gym (morning), cricket on weekends, vegetarian + egg + whey.
**Daily targets:** ~150–165 g protein · ~2,300–2,500 kcal · 4–5 L water.
**The one rule to remember:** *one shake + a protein anchor at lunch + a protein anchor at dinner + eggs/yogurt at breakfast + one protein snack.* That combination lands ~150 g protein every day without counting.

## 2. The ADHD operating system

This is what makes the plan survive contact with a real week.

1. **Zero morning decisions.** Breakfast and lunch are pre-assigned per day (Section 5). You don't choose — you execute.
2. **One Sunday batch session** (cook does it) loads the whole week. Checklist in Section 6.
3. **The shake is the anchor habit.** Shaker bottle + scoop pre-measured sits by the door / fridge. If a whole day goes sideways, the shake alone protects ~26 g.
4. **Liquid protein for low-appetite mornings.** If gym or stimulant meds kill your morning hunger, the no-cook yogurt+whey bowl or the shake go down easily when solid food won't. Don't skip — under-eating early causes the 4 p.m. crash-and-binge.
5. **Pre-portioned snacks.** Roasted chana / makhana / almonds bagged in single servings on Sunday. No "open the big packet" trap.
6. **Levers, not recalculations** (Section 7). If a day runs short, you pull a lever (+1 shake or +3 eggs). You never re-plan.

## 6. Sunday batch-prep checklist (for the cook)

- [ ] **Boil 12–14 eggs**, keep in fridge (covers breakfasts + snacks).
- [ ] **Ferment a big batch of idli/dosa batter** (Sat night) + grind **pesarattu** (whole moong) and **adai** (mixed dal) batters; all keep 2–4 days. This is the engine for the week's breakfasts.
- [ ] **Soak + sprout legumes** (moong, chana) instead of boiling raw — easier on the gut, less gas.
- [ ] **Soak + portion soya, rajma, chana** for the days they appear; pressure-cook day-of or night-before. Add a pinch of **hing + ginger** to every legume dish.
- [ ] **Hung curd + fresh curd** set from a litre of milk; keep a jar of **buttermilk** ready.
- [ ] **Sundal base**: boil and portion chana/peanuts for evening snacks.
- [ ] **Soak 1 tsp methi seeds** each night for morning (gut + digestion).
- [ ] **Snacks pre-portioned**: roasted chana, makhana + almonds into single-serve dabbas.
- [ ] **Whey scoop pre-measured** in the shaker, left by the fridge each night.
- [ ] **Veg chopped** for the week, including greens (keerai) and a raw banana for resistant starch.
- [ ] Each evening: cook tomorrow's lunch anchor + carb so it only needs reheating.

## 7. Adjustment levers (no recalculation needed)

- **Short ~25 g protein on a day?** Pull one: +1 lean shake / +3 boiled eggs / +100 g paneer / +50 g dry soya.
- **Want faster fat loss?** Drop one carb serving (rice or roti) at dinner and use the Lean shake only. Don't cut below ~3 protein anchors/day.
- **Want to add muscle / had a heavy session?** Switch the shake to **Mass**, add a carb at lunch.
- **Travelling / no kitchen?** Shake + boiled eggs + roasted chana = ~45 g in zero prep.

## 8. Gut health layer

You're eating a lot of dal, soya, rajma and whey, which is great for protein but hard on the gut if you don't build the microbiome alongside it. Four moves:

**1. A fermented food every day (probiotics).** Already built into the grid: idli/dosa/adai/pesarattu batters are naturally fermented; plus curd, buttermilk, neer mor and curd rice. The standout traditional option to add is **pazhaya sadam / pazhamkanji** — leftover cooked rice soaked in water overnight, eaten next morning with curd, raw onion and green chilli (add a boiled egg for protein). It's one of the most probiotic-dense things in South Indian cooking and needs zero effort.

**2. Prebiotics to feed those bacteria.** Onion, garlic and ginger in cooking; **raw banana (vazhakkai)** poriyal; **cooked-and-cooled rice** (curd rice, pazhaya sadam) for resistant starch; soaked **methi seeds**; a tbsp of soaked **chia or halim (garden cress)** stirred into curd.

**3. Kill the legume gas.** This matters at your protein intake. **Sprout** moong/chana before cooking, always add **hing (asafoetida) + ginger** to dals and legume curries, and ramp fibre up over 2–3 weeks rather than overnight so the gut adapts.

**4. Built-in digestives.** **Rasam** (pepper–cumin–tamarind) with lunch is anti-bloat; **ginger buttermilk** after a heavy meal; **saunf or jeera water** to close. 

**Diversity beats quantity:** aim for 30+ different plants a week — rotate dals, millets (ragi, foxtail), greens (keerai), veg and seeds. A varied microbiome comes from variety, not volume. Keep water at 4–5 L; under-hydration plus high protein is the usual constipation cause.

## 9. Notes

- **Whey:** plain/unflavoured or isolate if you bloat with concentrate. 1–2 scoops/day is plenty; food does the rest.
- **Appetite + meds:** if you're on a stimulant for ADHD, morning solid food may be hard — lean on the shake and yogurt bowl, and front-load nothing you'll skip. This isn't medical advice; if appetite suppression is significant, raise it with your doctor.
- **Cricket Saturday:** more carbs around the match, chaas/electrolytes during play, protein + carbs within an hour after.
- This plan assumes no medical restrictions. If you have any kidney, metabolic, or other conditions, run the protein level past a doctor or a registered dietitian first.
